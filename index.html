Frequently Asked Questions

Payments can generally be made online at the time of booking using a variety of methods. These typically include credit and debit cards (Visa, MasterCard, American Express), as well as popular online payment systems such as PayPal. Some hotels may also accept payments via bank transfers or digital wallets like Google Pay or Apple Pay.
In many cases, you also have the option to pay later or directly at the hotel during check-in. However, this depends on the hotel's policies and the type of rate you choose. Prepaid, non-refundable bookings require upfront payment at the time of booking, while flexible bookings may allow for payment upon arrival.
Make sure to check the payment details and cancellation terms carefully before completing your booking to avoid any surprises.

Once you complete your booking, you will receive a confirmation email from the hotel booking platform or directly from the hotel. This email contains important information such as your booking reference number, the hotel’s contact details, and the dates of your stay.
It’s a good idea to keep a copy of this email and the booking reference in case you need to make any changes or provide proof of your reservation upon check-in.
If you do not receive a confirmation email within a few minutes of completing your booking, check your spam or junk email folder. You can also log in to your account on the booking website to view your booking details under "My Bookings" or contact customer support to confirm your reservation.

Yes, you can make a booking for someone else using your account, provided you enter their details as the guest during the booking process.
While booking, simply fill in the name of the person who will be staying at the hotel in the "Guest Information" section. You can use your own payment information to complete the booking, even if the guest’s name is different from the cardholder's name.
It’s important that the guest arrives with a copy of the booking confirmation and their own ID to match the reservation details. Additionally, some hotels may require a credit card for incidentals, so the guest should bring their own card, even if the booking was paid for by someone else.

> Yes, cancellations and modifications to your booking are usually possible, but they depend on the cancellation policy of the hotel and the rate type you have selected.
Most hotels offer a flexible booking option, which allows for free cancellation or changes up to a certain period before the check-in date, often 24 to 48 hours prior. Non-refundable bookings, on the other hand, generally don’t allow for cancellations or changes without incurring a fee, which could be up to the full cost of the stay.
To cancel or modify a booking, log in to your account, navigate to "My Bookings," and select the option to cancel or change the details. Make sure to review the hotel's cancellation terms, as some might have stricter policies, especially during peak seasons.

Yes, there may be additional taxes or fees on top of the room rate. These can include local city taxes, resort fees, or service charges. These fees vary depending on the location of the hotel and its category.
For example, many cities charge a tourist tax or occupancy tax, which is either included in the room rate or added separately during booking. Resort hotels might charge a resort fee, which covers amenities such as Wi-Fi, pool access, and fitness centers. These fees can range from $10 to $40 per night.
The total cost of your stay, including taxes and fees, is usually shown during the booking process before you confirm your reservation. Be sure to review this breakdown so that you are aware of any additional charges you might need to pay either at the time of booking or at the hotel.
